# References — Cognitive Load Is the Missing Layer in Developer Experience

This document contains full citation details and source links for the references cited in the article.

All sources were selected to ground claims in established research rather than opinion.

---

## Cognitive Load Theory

**Sweller, J. (1988).**  
Cognitive load during problem solving: Effects on learning. *Cognitive Science, 12(2), 257–285.*  
https://doi.org/10.1207/s15516709cog1202_4

Foundational paper introducing Cognitive Load Theory and distinguishing intrinsic and extraneous load.

---

## Working Memory Capacity

**Miller, G. A. (1956).**  
The magical number seven, plus or minus two: Some limits on our capacity for processing information. *Psychological Review, 63(2), 81–97.*  
https://doi.org/10.1037/h0043158

Classic paper establishing working memory limits.  
Note: More recent research suggests effective working memory capacity may be closer to ~4 chunks in novel contexts.

---

## Interruption Costs

**Mark, G., Gudith, D., & Klocke, U. (2008).**  
The cost of interrupted work: More speed and stress. *Proceedings of the SIGCHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems.*  
https://doi.org/10.1145/1357054.1357072

Empirical research on interruption recovery time and task-switching costs in knowledge work environments.

---

## Executive Function & ADHD

**Barkley, R. A. (2012).**  
*Executive Functions: What They Are, How They Work, and Why They Evolved.* Guilford Press.

Comprehensive overview of executive function and working memory variability in ADHD.

---

## Burnout Research

**Maslach, C., & Leiter, M. P. (2016).**  
Understanding the burnout experience: Recent research and its implications for psychiatry. *World Psychiatry, 15(2), 103–111.*  
https://doi.org/10.1002/wps.20311

Defines burnout as a predictable outcome of chronic workplace stress and overload.

---

## Developer Productivity Research

**Forsgren, N., Humble, J., & Kim, G. (2018).**  
*Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps.* IT Revolution Press.

Research-backed analysis of engineering performance metrics and organizational impact.
