# References — There Is No Black Box

This document contains full citation details and DOI links for all references used in the article. Each source directly supports claims about cognitive limits, abstraction, modularity, overload, and bounded rationality.

---

## Cognitive Load & Working Memory

Sweller, J. (1988).  
Cognitive load during problem solving: Effects on learning.  
*Cognitive Science, 12(2), 257–285.*  
https://doi.org/10.1207/s15516709cog1202_4  

Foundational paper introducing Cognitive Load Theory and distinguishing intrinsic and extraneous load.

---

Miller, G. A. (1956).  
The magical number seven, plus or minus two: Some limits on our capacity for processing information.  
*Psychological Review, 63(2), 81–97.*  
https://doi.org/10.1037/h0043158  

Classic research on working memory limits. Later research refines the capacity estimate downward, but this remains foundational.

---

## Interruption & Cognitive Fragmentation

Mark, G., Gudith, D., & Klocke, U. (2008).  
The cost of interrupted work: More speed and stress.  
*Proceedings of the SIGCHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems.*  
https://doi.org/10.1145/1357054.1357072  

Empirical study demonstrating interruption recovery time and the cognitive cost of task switching in knowledge work environments.

---

## Encapsulation & Modular Design

Parnas, D. L. (1972).  
On the criteria to be used in decomposing systems into modules.  
*Communications of the ACM, 15(12), 1053–1058.*  
https://doi.org/10.1145/361598.361623  

Foundational paper on modularity and encapsulation in software engineering.

---

## Burnout & Sustained Overload

Maslach, C., & Leiter, M. P. (2016).  
Understanding the burnout experience: Recent research and its implications for psychiatry.  
*World Psychiatry, 15(2), 103–111.*  
https://doi.org/10.1002/wps.20311  

Grounds claims about sustained overload and burnout as predictable system outcomes.

---

## High-Performance Engineering Systems

Forsgren, N., Humble, J., & Kim, G. (2018).  
*Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps.*  
IT Revolution Press.  

Research-backed analysis of engineering performance metrics and system-level optimization.

---

## Bounded Rationality & Perspective Limits

Simon, H. A. (1956).  
Rational choice and the structure of the environment.  
*Psychological Review, 63(2), 129–138.*  
https://doi.org/10.1037/h0042769  

Introduces bounded rationality — the idea that humans operate under information and cognitive constraints rather than perfect knowledge.
